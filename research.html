Research

R1: Carbon Capture towards Net-Zero Future

Develop carbon capture processes using renewable energy sources that are robust, reliable, and scalable. Our particular interest is in direct air capture, which is essentially a net-negative emission method that effectively removes CO2 from the air.

R2: Carbon Conversion towards Energy Sustainability

Develop electrochemical carbon conversion processes. Our particular interest is in the development of direct capture and conversion, as well as the conversion of captured CO2 into value-added compounds such as fuels and nutrients.

R3: Electroactive Materials

Our research in electrochemical decarbonization engineering will lead us to develop new electroactive materials. Our main focus is on molecular organic and inorganic materials, but we are not limited to these.
